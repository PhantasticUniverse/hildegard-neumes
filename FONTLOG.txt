FONTLOG for Hildegard Neumes

This file provides detailed information on the Hildegard Neumes font.
This information should be distributed along with the Hildegard Neumes
font and any derivative works.

Basic Font Information
----------------------

Hildegard Neumes is an OpenType font for the Rhineland neume notation
used in Hildegard von Bingen's music manuscripts — the Dendermonde Codex
(c. 1174/75) and the Wiesbaden Riesencodex Hs. 2 (c. 1180–85). It is
designed as a drop-in replacement for the experimental inline-Rust
Rhineland glyph module in the Rhena/Viriditas notation platform, and
as a universally consumable OpenType artifact for any chant tool that
wants paleographically faithful 12th-century German neume shapes.

The font's v1 atomic inventory contains 19 glyphs covering puncta,
virgae, quilismae, oriscus, strophicus, pressus, liquescents, clefs,
division marks, and connector lines. Multi-component neume families
(pes, flexa, climacus, torculus, porrectus, and the Hildegard-idiosyncratic
compounds) are assembled by the consumer's resolver from these atoms.

Glyph shapes are traced from direct reads of the manuscript facsimiles
at Wiesbaden HLB RheinMain (Riesencodex, CC-BY 4.0) and the IMSLP
Dendermonde facsimile (public domain), following the paleographic
register documented in Gardiner 2022, van Poucke's Alamire facsimile
introduction, and direct folio inspection.

ChangeLog
---------

26 April 2026 (v0.2.0-alpha.2) — Hildegard Neumes Project maintainers — pre-release tag: contract-v2 schema (origin enum, liquescent_direction, x/y extents)

This pre-release cuts the contract-v2 schema agreed with the Rhena/Viriditas
team in the rhena-coordination thread (2026-04-26). No glyph inventory change
(still 25 atoms, same 4 placeholder atoms pending trace). Generated
rhineland.rs is byte-identical to v0.2.0-alpha.1 output (verified) — the v2
additions are consumed by Rhena's contract parser directly, not by the
Glyph { name, codepoint } emission path. Width changes flow through OTF/hmtx
on next deploy and require no Rust regen on Rhena's side.

Schema bump (in docs/rhena-coordination/rhineland.contract.json)
- Top-level `version: 1` renamed to `schema_version: 2` (no consumer reads
  this field; rename is for clarity vs. font/OTF version).
- `coordinate_system` extended with `y_zero: "staff_midline"`,
  `staff_lines_du: [-375, -125, 125, 375]`, `staff_space_du: 250`. Records
  the reference frame the per-atom `origin` field is interpreted against.
- New top-level blocks `origin_definitions` and `liquescent_direction_definitions`
  with verbatim per-enum-value descriptions sourced from the rhena-coordination
  thread; downstream consumers parsing the contract get authoritative
  semantics without chasing the cross-repo discussion.

Per-atom additive fields (4 new fields × 25 atoms; non-breaking)
- `liquescent_direction`: "ascending" | "descending" | "diminutive" | null.
  Three atoms tagged: rh_liquescent_asc → ascending, rh_liquescent_desc →
  descending, rh_deminutum → diminutive. Other 22 atoms null. Replaces
  Rhena's v1-stopgap of name-string sniffing.
- `origin`: "pitch-anchor" | "staff-fixed" | "composed". Per-atom split
  17 / 5 / 3:
    pitch-anchor (17): rh_punctum, rh_virga, rh_punctum_inclinatum,
      rh_quilisma, rh_oriscus, rh_strophicus, rh_pressus,
      rh_liquescent_asc, rh_liquescent_desc, rh_deminutum, rh_c_clef,
      rh_f_clef, rh_pes_short, rh_pes_foot, rh_pes_head, rh_flexa_short,
      rh_flexa_head.
    staff-fixed (5): rh_divisio_minima, rh_divisio_maior,
      rh_divisio_maxima, rh_divisio_finalis, rh_virgula.
    composed (3): rh_pes_line, rh_flexa_line, rh_flexa_foot.
  flexa_foot kept `composed` (origin = stem_top join, pitch_bottom inside
  the body at y=-119 exposed via compose_anchors) rather than rebaselined
  to pitch-anchor — preserves the descending-gesture model and the
  existing trace-at-scale geometry. v3 rebaseline deferred contingent on
  Rhena's composition pass actually proving frictional with the asymmetry.
- `x_extents: {min, max}` and `y_extents: {min, max}`: visible bbox of
  the atom's geometry in local em frame, computed from on-curve + control
  points in src/hildegard-neumes.ufo/glyphs/*.glif. Lets Rhena compute
  syllable-text placement and rollover wrap candidates from visible
  geometry rather than the advance box (matters for asymmetric atoms
  like rh_virga: x_extents [-44, 47] inside advance 120). Both fields
  are `null` for the 4 placeholder atoms (rh_oriscus, rh_deminutum,
  rh_f_clef, rh_liquescent_asc) — extents not measured because geometry
  is not yet traced. Rhena's consumer must handle null gracefully (likely
  fall back to advance-box layout, matching v1 behaviour).

Schema-bump deferrals (out of scope)
- `stem_extent_hint{min, max}`: declined by Rhena; their policy is "trust
  anchor distances, stretch pes_line/flexa_line between them." Re-proposed
  only if extreme-ambitus aesthetic artefacts surface during integration.

Coordination
- See contract-v2 thread on hildegard-neumes (issue link tbd; will move to
  Rhena side after ADR-0003 ownership migration).
- Rhena adoption ADR (filed by main project) references this contract +
  ADR-0021 three-class taxonomy (Class A / atom + anchor compositional).
- Liquescent trace priority next: rh_liquescent_asc ≈ rh_deminutum >
  rh_oriscus > rh_f_clef. Targeting v0.2.0-alpha.3.

20 April 2026 (v0.2.0-alpha.1) — Hildegard Neumes Project maintainers — pre-release tag: pes/flexa compositional families, ADR-0012 alignment, 25-atom contract

This is the first pre-release tag, cutting the font for Rhena integration
under ADR-0012 (@font-face + codepoint delivery). 21 of 25 atoms are
finalized Rhineland traces; 4 (oriscus, liquescent_asc/epiphonus,
deminutum, f_clef) ship as placeholder rectangles until reference images
can be traced, and will be superseded in v0.2.0 or later alpha iterations.

Atom inventory: 19 → 25
- rh_pes_short, rh_pes_foot, rh_pes_head added as a compositional pes
  family alongside the existing rh_pes_line. rh_pes_short is an
  integrated one-gesture glyph for the 2nd interval; rh_pes_foot +
  stretched rh_pes_line + rh_pes_head compose wider intervals.
  Paleographic fidelity per ADR-0008 — Hildegard pes bottom is a hook
  (not a punctum) and its top is a head without a virga-terminator
  hook. The integrated short variant is required for 2nds because the
  combined foot/head stem stubs exceed a 125-du 2nd interval.
- rh_flexa_short, rh_flexa_head, rh_flexa_foot added paralleling the
  pes family for descending compounds. rh_flexa_line redesigned from
  the abandoned-rhineland.rs 172-du diagonal parallelogram to a
  vertical stem after paleographic review of the tall-flexa manuscript
  reference confirmed Hildegard's flexa stem is vertical, not diagonal.

New contract metadata for compositional atoms
- compose_anchors: each compositional atom declares pitch anchors
  (pitch_top / pitch_bottom) and stem-joint anchors (stem_top /
  stem_bottom for foot+head atoms, base/tip for _line atoms) in
  atom-local Y-up em-square coordinates (1000 UPM). Pitch anchors
  normalized so pitch_x == stem_x within each family (pes=56,
  flexa=50); composed stems align automatically in a single vertical
  column with no per-family branching or inter-note offset needed in
  the consumer's resolver.
- stem_channel { x_min, x_max }: added to foot / head / _line atoms
  as the x-band of the calligraphic stem. Enables Rhena's ADR-0012
  renderer to draw native SVG <line> connectors with correct position
  and thickness. Pes: x=[36, 76] (40 du thick). Flexa: x=[31, 69]
  (38 du thick).

Codepoint assignment — SMuFL extension block U+F400..F40F
- All 9 Rhineland non-SMuFL atoms consolidated into a single block for
  Verovio / LilyPond / @font-face interop: rh_pes_short U+F400,
  rh_pes_foot U+F401, rh_pes_head U+F402, rh_pressus U+F403 (stable),
  rh_pes_line U+F404, rh_flexa_short U+F405, rh_flexa_head U+F406,
  rh_flexa_foot U+F407, rh_flexa_line U+F408. SMuFL-mapped atoms
  (punctum, virga, clefs, divisios, virgula, etc.) keep their
  existing SMuFL codepoints.

Build pipeline
- just build-font now emits OTF + WOFF + WOFF2 from the single UFO3
  source (FontForge Python driver; dual WOFF flavour generation via
  font.generate() with extension inference). Rhena ADR-0012 expects
  both WOFFs served via @font-face with WOFF2 preferred and WOFF as
  a legacy SVG-consumer fallback.
- scripts/generate-rhena-glyphs.py rewritten against the ADR-0012
  contract. Path-extraction machinery retired; the script now emits
  two parallel Rust tables from the authoritative contract JSON:
  Glyph { name, codepoint: char } for all 25 atoms, and
  CompositionalMetadata { name, compose_anchors, stem_channel } for
  the 8 pes/flexa family atoms that participate in composition.
  The Glyph struct stays pure-rendering-primitive (no compose_anchors
  None boilerplate on non-compositional atoms), per Rhena v2 feedback.
  codepoint emits as Rust char literal ('\u{F401}'), matching Rhena's
  existing pub codepoint: char type from ADR-0012; no chant.rs churn
  on adoption.
- Generator supports SOURCE_DATE_EPOCH for reproducible @generated-header
  timestamps; default is wall-clock UTC. just check-generated pins the
  epoch for CI determinism.
- Output path renamed generated/rhineland_glyphs.rs →
  generated/rhineland.rs to match Rhena's adoption destination
  (crates/rhena-core/src/render_ir/glyphs/rhineland.rs), reducing
  adoption friction to a single verbatim file copy.

Known limitations / follow-ups
- scripts/validate-font.py and tests/test_*.py were written for the
  path-extraction codegen and reference the old 19-atom inventory;
  they haven't been updated for the ADR-0012 metadata emission or
  the 25-atom contract. Validation will run fine against the OTF
  glyph inventory check but its SVG-path-command validation is
  obsolete. Test suite refactor deferred to a post-alpha pass.
- 4 placeholder-rectangle atoms still need paleographic references
  and traces: rh_oriscus, rh_liquescent_asc, rh_deminutum, rh_f_clef.
  These are shipped as sized rectangles so consumers can integrate
  against the full 25-atom inventory; they'll be swapped for traces
  in a future alpha without contract changes.
- Flexa-of-3rd composition leaves only 6 du for the stretched
  rh_flexa_line (foot + head stubs total 244 du vs 250-du interval).
  Paleographically this is probably correct (Hildegard's 3rd-flexa
  has stems that nearly touch), but deserves a visual check against
  the Dendermonde facsimile once we have an integration render.

19 April 2026 (v0.1.0-dev) — Hildegard Neumes Project maintainers — trace-at-scale
- Adopted trace-at-scale methodology for Phase C calligraphic atoms.
  With 12 staff-space horizontal guidelines now visible in every
  glyph editor (y ∈ ±250, ±500, ±750 whole staff spaces; ±125, ±375,
  ±625 half staff spaces), the authoring workflow for each glyph is:
    1. Import the manuscript reference image to the Background layer
    2. Scale the image so its own staff lines align with our font's
       staff-space guidelines (calibrating glyph-to-staff ratio by
       construction)
    3. Trace the glyph on Fore at that calibrated scale
    4. Measure the resulting body bbox
    5. Set the contract advance width from the measurement
  This supersedes "draw to the brief's stated dimensions" — paleographic
  measurement is now the contract authority per ADR-0008.
- First two glyphs re-measured via trace-at-scale:
  - rh_punctum 240 → 160 du (body bbox 130 × 71 du, asymmetric
    body+tail). Matches Bravura chantPunctum exactly.
  - rh_virga 240 → 120 du (head 91 wide, stem + foot total height
    186 du). Narrower than the punctum when traced side-by-side at
    the same staff scale — earlier "virga head ≈ punctum breadth"
    was close-enough eyeball but traced measurement shows the virga
    head is about 70% of punctum width.
- src/hildegard-neumes.ufo/glyphs/rh_punctum.glif and rh_virga.glif
  replaced with the scale-corrected traces. widths.json, contract
  file, glyph_priority_sheet.md, README width table, scaffold-ufo.py
  PLACEHOLDER_BBOX, and paleographic briefs §§ 1 & 2 updated.
- Remaining calligraphic atoms (punctum_inclinatum, quilisma, oriscus,
  strophicus, pressus, liquescents, deminutum, clefs) will be measured
  via trace-at-scale as drawn.

19 April 2026 (v0.1.0-dev) — Hildegard Neumes Project maintainers — paleographic correction
- rh_virga advance widened 90 → 240 du after direct manuscript
  comparison. The user photographed a virga and a punctum side-by-side
  on the same folio; the virga head was clearly the same breadth as
  the adjacent punctum, not the narrow ~90-du head our brief and
  contract had specified. Bravura follows the same convention
  (chantPunctum and chantPunctumVirga both 160 du in its scale), so
  our correction aligns with the SMuFL reference as well.
- Paleographic brief § 2 rewritten: head is now "a small punctum on
  top of a stem" — same calligraphic character and breadth as
  rh_punctum, descending into a thin stem with a small foot hook.
  Body bbox updated to (-100, -560) to (+120, +50).
- Widths contract (src/widths.json and docs/rhena-coordination/
  rhineland.contract.json), scaffold placeholder bbox, glyph priority
  sheet, and README width table all updated to match.
- Per ADR-0008 paleographic fidelity policy: direct manuscript
  inspection is the authority for visual-shape questions. The earlier
  90-du value was inherited from the abandoned rhineland.rs (65) and
  bumped via bbox-only width review without paleographic verification
  against manuscript sources.
